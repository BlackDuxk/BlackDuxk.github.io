Overview

What Is Book Banning?

Book banning is a type of censorship that prohibits or limits the free access to content within a piece of literature or printed works. In this instance printed works can be considered as work such as: essays, plays, magazines, novels, and etc.

The Difference Between Challenging An Banning

Challenging a book is an attempt to remove or restrict access to the contents in that book based on an objection made by a member of the public, normally a parent or teacher. Banning a book is the action of using legislation to restrict access to that book and its contents from public.

Why Are Books Banned?

Books are banned for a variety of different reasons, however the most common reasons is to protect others, spicifically children, from content that incude:

  • Racial Issues
  • Encouraging a Damaging Lifestyle
  • Blasphemous Dialog
  • Sexual or Explict Situaitons or Dialog
  • Violence or Negativity
  • Presence of Witchcraft
  • Unpopular Religious Affiliations
  • Political Bias
  • Age Inapproptiate Content

How'd Book Banning Start?

The first bannning of a book can be traced back to an act that is more commonly refered to as book burning. Book burning originated in 1933 by the Nazi's during the Holocaust. During the Holocaust Hitler ordered roughly 25,000 books to be burned based on their content being "unGerman".
